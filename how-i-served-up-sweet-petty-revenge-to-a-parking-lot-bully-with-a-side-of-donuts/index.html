    How I Served Up Sweet Petty Revenge to a Parking Lot Bully (With a Side of Donuts)

    Let’s be honest: College parking lots are the real-life Hunger Games. If you’ve ever circled a campus lot like a hungry shark, praying for someone to leave, you know the desperation is real. But nothing brings out the worst in humanity quite like the hunt for a coveted parking spot—and sometimes, karma needs a little help finding its way.

    Recently, I stumbled across a story on Reddit’s r/PettyRevenge that perfectly captures the chaos, comedy, and comeuppance of campus parking wars. It’s a tale of frustration, quick thinking, and the soul-soothing power of donuts. Buckle up, because this is one ride you won’t want to miss.

    The Great College Parking Showdown

    Redditor u/xPearlRosie sets the scene: College parking is always a struggle, but on this particular day, things took a turn for the dramatic. After a long day of classes, our hero and a friend were making the trek back to their cars—early birds who had snagged the rarest of treasures: good parking spots.

    Enter the antagonist. Imagine a driver so desperate for a space that she’s tailing them through the lot, practically breathing down their necks. Her intent is clear: She wants their spots, and she isn’t afraid to make it uncomfortable. If you’ve ever felt the pressure of a lurking car as you fumble with your keys, you know the vibe—part intimidation, part impatience, all annoyance.

    Usually, our narrator is a “live and let live” kind of person. But when someone turns up the aggression, sometimes you’re left with no choice but to fight back… in the pettiest way possible.

    Plotting the Perfect Petty Payback

    As fate would have it, both friends were parked in the same area. That’s when inspiration struck. With a conspiratorial glance, they both realized: We don’t actually need to leave right now. So why not make this pushy parker’s day a little more… interesting?

    Instead of hopping in and vacating their coveted spots, they chucked their bags in their cars and struck up a leisurely conversation. Thirty minutes of animated chit-chat ensued, with the desperate driver hovering—and fuming—the entire time. It was a masterclass in passive resistance.

    Best of all? Donuts were involved. Because if you’re going to hold your ground, you might as well have snacks.

    Why This Works: The Sweet Science of Petty Revenge

    Let’s break it down: Why is this story so satisfying? For starters, it’s a classic underdog moment. In the lawless world of college parking, the only rule is “every driver for themselves.” But when someone crosses the line from eager to aggressive, it’s only fair to serve up a little poetic justice.

    The beauty of this revenge is its simplicity. No damage done, no rules broken—just a well-timed conversation and a refusal to be bullied. It’s the kind of payback that’s more about reclaiming dignity than doling out actual harm.

    And let’s not overlook the donuts. Not only do they make a stakeout more enjoyable, but they also add a touch of whimsy to the whole affair. “Oh, you want my spot? Sorry, I’m busy enjoying this maple bar with my friend. Maybe next time, try a little patience.”

    Parking Lot Etiquette: A Lost Art?

    This story also highlights a bigger issue: People can get weirdly aggressive over parking spots. There’s an unspoken etiquette—if you see someone walking to their car, maybe hang back a bit. Don’t tailgate, don’t honk, and for the love of all things holy, don’t act like you’re in Fast & Furious: Campus Drift.

    When someone does cross the line, a little creative resistance isn’t just justified—it’s necessary. It reminds everyone that common courtesy still counts, even in the wilds of college parking lots.

    The Moral of the Story? Don’t Be a Parking Lot Jerk

    Next time you’re tempted to hound someone for their spot, remember: You never know when your target might be armed with a petty plan and a box of donuts. Sometimes, the best revenge is served warm, glazed, and with a side of small talk.

    So, have you ever served up your own petty parking lot justice? Or maybe you’ve been on the receiving end of someone else’s creative revenge? Share your stories in the comments! And if you’re heading to college next semester, remember—pack your patience… and maybe some snacks, just in case.

    Because in the end, it’s not just about the parking spot. It’s about standing your ground—and having a little fun while you do it.
